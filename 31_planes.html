31 Planes of Existence

Buddhist Cosmology & Rebirth

The Vertical Cosmos
From Hell (Niraya) to Formless Bliss (Arupa)

In Buddhist cosmology, the universe is stratified into 31 distinct planes of existence. Beings are reborn into these planes based on their Karma (actions). None of these realms are permanent; even life in the highest heavens eventually ends, leading to rebirth elsewhere, unless one attains Nirvana.

The Formless Sphere (Arupa-loka)

The highest realms, purely mental. Beings here have no physical bodies, only consciousness. Rebirth here is achieved through advanced meditation (Jhanas).

The Form Sphere (Rupa-loka)

Realms of fine material form. Beings (Brahmas) have light, ethereal bodies and exist in states of sublime bliss. They are free from sensual desire/lust.

The Sensuous Sphere (Kama-loka)

Realms dominated by five senses and desire. Includes hells, humans, and lower heavens.
