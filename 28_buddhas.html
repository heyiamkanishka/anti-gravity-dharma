The 28 Buddhas

The Lineage of the Awakened Ones

Lineage of Light

In the Theravada Buddhist tradition, the Buddhavamsa represents a lineage of 28 Buddhas. While the Dhamma is eternal, it is lost to humanity over vast periods of time. A "Buddha" (Awakened One) is a rare being who rediscovers this truth unaided and teaches it to the world. Siddhartha Gautama is simply the most recent in this infinite succession.

The lineage is divided into three eras based on the Kalpa (eon) in which they appeared.

1. Taṇhaṅkara

Era: Sari-Maṇḍa Kalpa (4 Asankheyyas + 100,000 kalpas ago)

The first of the ancient Buddhas. He attained enlightenment sitting under a Rukkaththana tree.

2. Medhaṅkara

Era: Sari-Maṇḍa Kalpa

He attained enlightenment under a Kaela tree. His light was said to outshine the sun.

3. Saraṇaṅkara

Era: Sari-Maṇḍa Kalpa

Known as the "Refuge Creator," he attained enlightenment under a Pulila tree.

4. Dīpaṅkara

Era: Sari-Maṇḍa Kalpa

Significance: It was at the feet of Dipankara Buddha that the ascetic Sumedha (the future Siddhartha Gautama) first made the vow to become a Buddha himself. Dipankara prophesied his future success.

...The Intermediate Buddhas...

Kondañña, Maṅgala, Sumana, Revata, Sobhita, Anomadassi, Paduma, Narada, Padumuttara, Sumedha, Sujata, Piyadassi, Atthadassi, Dhammadassi, Siddhattha, Tissa, Phussa, Vipassi, Sikhi, Vessabhu

Across countess eons, these Buddhas appeared, turned the Wheel of Dharma, and passed into Parinirvana, keeping the torch of truth alive.

25. Kakusandha

Era: Bhadda Kalpa (Current Eon)

The first Buddha of our current fortunate eon (Bhadda Kalpa), which is unique for having five Buddhas.

26. Koṇāgamana

Era: Bhadda Kalpa (Current Eon)

The second Buddha of this eon. He is believed to have been born in a time when human lifespan was significantly longer.

27. Kassapa

Era: Bhadda Kalpa (Current Eon)

The third Buddha of this eon. He lived immediately before Gautama. His stupas are still venerated in legends.

28. Gautama (Siddhartha)

Era: Bhadda Kalpa (Current Eon) - ~563 BCE

The Buddha of our time. He rediscovered the lost path and established the Sangha that exists today.

The Future: Metteyya (Maitreya)

Era: Future of Bhadda Kalpa

The 29th Buddha, predicted to appear in the distant future when the Dhamma has been completely forgotten. He will be known for his immense loving-kindness.
